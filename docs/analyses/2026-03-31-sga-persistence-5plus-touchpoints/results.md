# SGA Lead Handling Analysis — Q1 2026 Results

**Run date**: 2026-03-31
**Cohort**: Leads entering funnel Jan 1 – Mar 9, 2026 (aging bias cutoff)
**Activity window**: Jan 1 – Mar 31, 2026
**Population**: 16 active SGAs (excl. Lauren George and non-SGA users)

---

## Executive Summary

Across 20,723 Q1 leads assigned to active SGAs, **10.4% of unengaged worked leads received 5+ outbound touchpoints** (persistence), while **90.6% of closed-lost leads were abandoned with fewer than 5 touches** (premature abandonment). Nearly 1 in 5 leads (19.7%) received **zero tracked outbound activity**, with 2,091 of those closed without any outreach. Lead recycling is minimal (2.1% of leads touched by >1 SGA) and does not materially change attribution. **Multi-channel outbound coverage is 30.1% at 2+ channels when including automated email (lemlist/ListEmail).** Email penetration jumps from 0.2% (manual only) to 23.7% when including automated campaigns — the candidate receives the email regardless. A realistic OKR is 2+ channels at 40-45%, with 3+ channels as a stretch target. The biggest OKR leverage is reducing premature abandonment — 4,462 leads were closed after just 1-4 touches with no reply/engagement, and the top 3 SGAs prove that 20%+ persistence is achievable.

**Correction notes (v2)**: Two corrections applied vs original results: (1) "Not Interested in Moving" disposition leads reclassified as Replied/Engaged — 635 leads moved, reflecting that these candidates communicated disinterest even when no inbound Task record was tracked. (2) Email channel in Metric 5 expanded to include automated campaigns (lemlist/ListEmail) — for channel presence (did we reach this person?), automated email counts.

## 7. Key Observations

1. **The dominant story is early quitting**: 57.8% of all Q1 leads (11,981) were worked with 1-4 touches, got no response or engagement, and the SGA moved on. This is the single largest bucket and the biggest OKR lever. Even moving 10% of these to 5+ touches would nearly double the persistence rate.

2. **Top 3 SGAs prove 20%+ persistence is achievable**: Jason Ainsworth (28.0%), Ryan Crandall (23.8%), and Holly Huffman (23.1%) show that the 5-touch threshold is reachable. Their abandonment rates (68-74%) are also the lowest — persistence and reduced abandonment go hand-in-hand.

3. **The coverage gap (19.7%) includes a data quality concern**: 1,656 leads are marked "Contacted" but have zero tracked outbound touches. This is either untracked outreach (personal phones, unsynced dialers) or stage manipulation. Before setting a coverage OKR, RevOps should investigate.

4. **"No Response" is the dominant close reason (3,022 leads)**: This is the most addressable cohort — these leads went silent and the SGA gave up. "Auto-Closed by Operations" (2,414) represents system cleanup and may not be SGA-controllable. "Not Interested in Moving" (1,910) is now correctly classified as engaged — the candidate responded.

5. **Volume vs persistence is a real tradeoff**: Russell Armitage has the highest reply count (369) and most worked leads (1,870) but the lowest persistence (3.3%) and highest abandonment (97.1%). His breadth-first approach generates engagement at volume but leaves many leads insufficiently worked.

6. **Brian O'Hara is the most intriguing near-miss**: Avg 3.7 touchpoints on unengaged leads (second-highest) but only 3.6% hit 5+. He's touching leads frequently but not quite pushing past the threshold. Targeted coaching could move his number significantly.

7. **The 1,685 zero-touch still-open leads are effectively neglected**: These entered the funnel before Mar 10 and have received no tracked outreach after 3+ weeks. These should be flagged for immediate SGA action or redistribution.

8. **Recycling is a non-issue**: Only 2.1% of leads show multi-SGA activity, and the per-SGA persistence deltas are typically <0.5pp. Lead-owner attribution is defensible.

9. **Multi-channel coverage is at 30.1% with automated email included**: Including lemlist/ListEmail in the Email channel raised email penetration from 0.2% to 23.7%, and 2+ channel rate from 15.3% to 30.1%. This is a meaningful OKR baseline. The biggest channel gaps are now Call (8.2%) and LinkedIn (10.0%).

10. **Automated email drives most email coverage but is not SGA effort**: ~90% of email touches are lemlist campaigns, not manual SGA emails. This is appropriate for "candidate experience" (multi-channel coverage) but should not be double-counted as SGA effort in the persistence metric.

11. **Combined OKR recommendation: "5+ touches across 2+ channels"**: With 30.1% at 2+ channels and 10.4% persistence, the ideal Q2 OKR combines both: persistent AND multi-channel. Among 5+ touch leads, 65.3% already use 2+ channels and 44.5% use 3+ channels. Target: 40-45% at 2+ channels team-wide, with 3+ channels (SMS + LinkedIn/Email + Call) as stretch.

12. **Council flags "Timing" (53) and "No Show" (19) dispositions**: Both imply candidate engagement but were not reclassified in this round. Consider adding them in the next iteration — small volume but logically consistent with the "Not Interested in Moving" treatment.

## Methodology Notes

- **Attribution**: Lead owner (`SGA_Owner_Name__c`), not task executor. Cross-SGA activity is 2.1% — immaterial at team OKR level.
- **Cohort cutoff**: Leads entering after Mar 9 excluded (aging bias control — leads need ~3 weeks to reach 5 touches).
- **Activity window**: Activities counted from each lead's FilterDate through Mar 31, 2026.
- **Outbound definition (touchpoint counting)**: Direction = 'Outbound' in `vw_sga_activity_performance` (SMS, LinkedIn, Calls, manual Email). Excludes: lemlist automated campaigns, ListEmail bulk sends, engagement tracking (click events).
- **Outbound definition (channel presence, Metric 5 only)**: Email channel includes ALL email activity (manual + lemlist + ListEmail). SMS/LinkedIn/Call presence still uses standard filtered definition. Rationale: for "did we reach this person?", automated email counts — the candidate receives it in their inbox.
- **Replied/Engaged**: Any `direction = 'Inbound'` activity excluding Marketing (form submissions), OR `Disposition__c = 'Not Interested in Moving'` (candidate communicated disinterest even without tracked inbound Task record). 1,269 of 1,910 NIM leads already had tracked inbound activity; 641 reclassified via disposition inference.
- **Closed lead indicator**: `lead_closed_date IS NOT NULL` — this is the Lead-level Stage_Entered_Closed timestamp.
- **Exclusion hierarchy**: Converted > MQL > Replied/Engaged (inbound activity OR disposition) > Unengaged (mutually exclusive).
- **Minimum activity**: "Worked" = >=1 outbound touchpoint on or after lead's FilterDate.
- **Waterfall**: Mutually exclusive buckets verified to sum to exactly 20,723.
- **Council reviewed**: OpenAI (gpt-5.4) and Gemini (gemini-3.1-pro-preview), 4 rounds. Full feedback in analysis-plan.md appendix.
- **Known data quality issue**: 1,656 leads are `is_contacted = 1` with 0 tracked outbound touches. Additionally, 256 "Not Interested in Moving" leads have zero tracked outbound but are now classified as Replied/Engaged — these likely represent untracked outreach. Flagged for RevOps investigation.
- **Disposition caveat**: Premature abandonment rate (90.6%) excludes "Not Interested in Moving" (engaged) but still includes "Bad Contact Info" and "Auto-Closed by Operations" (not SGA-controllable). Refine before OKR-setting.
- **Council open item**: Both reviewers flagged that lemlist SMS/LinkedIn/Call should logically also be included in channel presence for consistency with the email treatment. Current impact would be minimal (SMS already at 97.9%). Decision deferred to user.
