## 2. Deep Dives — Top 3 Competitors

### Farther (12 deals — CRITICAL THREAT)

**What they offer:**
- Equity ownership in the firm (advisors own a stake, not just their book)
- "Superior payouts" — exact rate undisclosed but positioned as industry-leading
- Full tech stack: automated tax-loss harvesting, asset location, rebalancing, CRM, multi-custodial (Schwab, Fidelity, Pershing, Apex)
- Zero non-competes, no mandatory minimums
- 100% ownership of client book with guaranteed buyout/succession
- Concierge onboarding team, back-office admin, compliance, marketing

**Our losses to them (12 deals, avg $72.9M AUM):**
- Loss reasons span the spectrum: Economics, Timing, Fear of Change, Lost to Competitor, Poor Culture Fit, Compliance
- One advisor cited a **$30K sign-on bonus** from Farther plus only $10M transferable AUM — economics didn't work for Savvy
- Another advisor was "considering another opportunity" and canceled before tech demo
- Partner influence: "His partner, Devin, was likely influencing him elsewhere"
- Pressure backfire: One $60.7M advisor "didn't like the pressure around start date"
- One advisor's "story continued to change" — bounced between Ameriprise, Savvy, and Farther

**Their momentum:**
- $72M Series C (CapitalG + Viewpoint Ventures), $542M valuation
- 5x AUM growth in 2024, now >$5B AUM
- 23 new advisors in H2 2024 alone
- Recent wins: $160M team (Jan 2026), $140M team (Feb 2026), $185M team (Mar 2026)
- Named AdvisorHub top recruiter of the year
- Claims 3X advisor book growth vs. industry average

**Their weakness:**
- Still relatively small (~100 advisors, $5B AUM) — limited brand recognition outside fintech circles
- VC-funded with no clear path to profitability — advisors take on startup risk
- No brick-and-mortar presence — one advisor specifically left for a local firm citing need for in-person mentoring
- Equity upside is speculative until IPO or exit event

**How to counter:**
- **Direct comparison deck** showing Savvy vs. Farther: highlight Savvy's established compliance infrastructure, local presence, and proven advisor retention
- **Speed**: Farther moves in 1.5 months — Savvy must match or beat this pace in the sales process
- **Address the equity pitch**: Quantify the risk/reward of Farther equity vs. Savvy's economics
- **Don't pressure on start dates** — lost a $60.7M deal this way

### LPL Financial (8 deals — HIGH THREAT)

**What they offer:**
- Largest IBD in the US (~23,000 advisors)
- Upfront forgivable loans: one advisor received **$114K–$120K upfront loan** option from LPL
- 2025 production bonus thresholds increased (4% bonus now requires $1M revenue, up from $750K)
- Full transition support team, compliance, training
- Multiple affiliation models: independent, employee, Strategic Wealth Services

**Our losses to them (8 deals, avg $25.6M AUM):**
- Economics is the dominant reason: "Most important thing is dollars when he walks in the door"
- One advisor had 70/30 BD/advisory split — couldn't underwrite with smaller advisory AUM
- Fear of Change and Book Not Transferable also cited
- These tend to be **smaller-book advisors** ($25.6M avg) who need upfront cash for transition

**Their weakness:**
- Raised bonus thresholds in 2025 — some advisors may be squeezed
- Scale means less personalized attention
- Technology often cited as dated vs. newer platforms
- Revenue share model less favorable than pure RIA economics long-term

**How to counter:**
- **Transition assistance fund**: Even a modest upfront payment ($50K–$100K) would neutralize LPL's biggest draw for sub-$50M advisors
- **Calculate the long-term math**: Show advisors that LPL's 10-year forgivable loan is effectively a handcuff, while Savvy's economics are better over 5+ years
- **Target their discontented**: LPL raised thresholds — advisors who just missed bonuses may be receptive

### Cetera (6 deals — HIGH THREAT)

**What they offer:**
- Up to **150 bps sign-on bonus** on advisory assets (60%+ advisory required) — e.g., $1.5M for a $100M advisor
- 9–10 year forgivable loan structure
- Multiple models: Wealth Services, Financial Institutions, Advisors
- Recent acquisitions adding capabilities (Darnall Sikes, $1.9B)
- ~12,000 advisors, $590B in assets

**Our losses to them (6 deals, avg $45M AUM):**
- Fear of Change and Timing dominate — these advisors aren't leaving for better economics, they're staying with the familiar
- One advisor "doesn't want to think about a move until fall"
- One $45M advisor simply "stopped responding"
- Compliance was a factor in one loss

**Their weakness:**
- Massive sign-on creates golden handcuffs (9–10 year amortization)
- Multiple entity structure creates confusion
- Post-M&A integration challenges (acquiring firms rapidly)
- Less tech-forward than Farther or Savvy

**How to counter:**
- **Highlight the 10-year trap**: Frame Cetera's sign-on as debt, not a bonus
- **Nurture the timing losses**: 3 of 6 Cetera losses were timing/fear — these advisors may re-engage in 6–12 months
- **Technology comparison**: Savvy's tech stack is a differentiator vs. Cetera's legacy infrastructure
